Keyword searches are as simple as typing words separated by a space, making sure not to enter any extra or unnecessary words. PACER Tracker only has the ability to filter cases by words in their titles. Here are some examples of titles for different types of cases:

  • Bankruptcy cases: 11-12345 John James Doe
  • Criminal cases: 4:13-cr-12345-1 USA v. Doe
  • Civil cases: 2:13-cv-12345 John Doe v. ACME Industries et al
  • Appeals cases: 13-1234 Doe v. USA

So, criminal cases and often appeals cases only list last names of parties, which means you can only filter by last name. PACER Tracker is not useful for finding criminal cases involving people with common last names. Bankruptcy cases do list the full name, though, and civil cases often contain the full name of one plaintiff and one defendant. Other parties ("et al") to a case are not listed, meaning that you can not filter an alert to return cases that list "New York City" as a second, third, etc. party.

Type in the case number and check the "Sample Results" tab above to see if it appears. If it does not appear or if the case is extremely new or hasn't been active in more than a year, check to make sure you have selected the correct court and then follow these guidelines to ensure PACER Tracker will send you an alert the next time the case is active.

PACER Tracker's case numbers may differ from those on court documents. So, instead of typing an entire case number, type its two-digit year, followed by a space, then its (usually) five-digit number, including any leading or trailing zeroes. So, for case "1:12-cv-01234-xxx," which would have been filed in 2012, type this in the alert box: 12 01234.

If your search returns more than one case, go ahead and type the whole case number into the search box or add the name of a party to a case. But remember, the more specific your search the less likely you are to receive an alert. It may be better to get two emails rather than none.
